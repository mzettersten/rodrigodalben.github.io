--- layout: page title: ManyBabies subtitle: Multi-lab replications of influential experiments in developmental psychology use-site-title: true nav-short: true bigimg: /img/curiosity-1910023_cut2.jpg --- **ManyBabies** is a collaborative project for replication and best practices in developmental psychology research. Our goal is to bring researchers together to address difficult outstanding theoretical and methodological questions about the nature of early development and how it is studied. For example: - Infants prefer helpers or hinders? - Infants prefer novel or familiar stimuli? - Is Infant Direct Speech is more appealing to infants than Adult Directed Speech? Learn more about **MB** origin, goals, and people [here]({{site.baseurl}}/about/). Check the latest [news]({{site.baseurl}}/news/) to stay up to date. ***

Projects

The broader goals of ManyBabies come together in a set of collaborative projects. Which can be of three types: main, spin-off, and secondary analysis. Learn more and see a list of ongoing projects here.

Main projects are listed below, please note that they can be Empiric or Methodologic in nature. Spin-off and secondary analysis for each project are contained under these pages.

Empiric


MB1: Infant-Directed Speech Preference

Do infants have a preference for infant-directed speech? How does this preference vary across age, method, and native language background? Learn more

MB2: Theory of Mind in Infancy

Do infants show anticipatory looking that reflects the false belief of another agent? Learn more

MB3: Rule Learning

Do infants learn and generalize identity-based rules from speech stimuli? Learn more

MB4: Moral Evaluation

Do infants prefer agents who are seen to help over those who are seen to hinder? Learn more

MB5: The Hunter & Ames model

What factors shape infants’ preferences for familiar and novel stimuli? Learn more

Methodologic


MB-AtHome

Work towards solutions for testing infants in their home, with a focus on measuring looks. Learn more
